Common Mistakes in Environmental Protection

The following are common mistakes people make when trying to protect the environment. Avoiding these can make a big difference in your impact.

Overusing Single-Use Plastics
Single-use plastics contribute significantly to pollution. Instead of relying on plastic bags, bottles, or utensils, opt for reusable alternatives whenever possible. View Example.
Wasting Energy at Home
Leaving lights, electronics, or appliances on when not in use wastes energy. Be mindful of your energy consumption by unplugging unused devices and switching to energy-efficient options. View Example.
Improper Recycling
Recycling incorrectly can cause contamination, leading entire batches of recyclables to be discarded. Always check local recycling guidelines to ensure you are sorting materials correctly. View Example.

For more tips on protecting the environment, visit the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).
